# Open Access Resources for Teaching Audio Mixing

A comprehensive research survey compiled for undergraduate lecture planning.

## 1. Free Multitrack Sessions & Stems for Practice

### Premier Resources

**Cambridge-MT / Mixing Secrets Free Multitrack Library**
- https://www.cambridge-mt.com/ms/mtk/
- 500+ free multitrack projects across many genres
- ZIP archives of uncompressed WAV files (24-bit/16-bit, 44.1kHz)
- Raw/unprocessed audio — no effects baked in
- "Edited Excerpt" versions (e.g., biggest chorus) ideal for classroom assignments
- Supports Mike Senior's book *Mixing Secrets for the Small Studio*
- Educational use only; no commercial use
- **Best single resource for teaching — massive, well-organized, genre-diverse**

**Telefunken "Live From The Lab"**
- https://www.telefunken-elektroakustik.com/multitracks/
- Free multitrack WAV downloads (24-bit/48kHz)
- All sessions tracked live with Telefunken microphones
- Good for teaching live recording issues (mic bleed, crosstalk)
- Educational/home studio use only

**LEWITT myLEWITT Free Multitracks**
- https://www.lewitt-audio.com/
- Free multitrack sessions from mixing contests (contests ended, stems still available)
- Includes sessions like Marina & The Kats (28-track neo swing), Saint Agnes (rock), Spitting Ibex
- Download via free myLEWITT account

**Produce Like A Pro (Warren Huart)**
- https://producelikeapro.com/blog/free-multitracks-hone-mixing-skills/
- Free multitracks available alongside free YouTube mixing tutorials
- Academy (paid) has 200+ multitrack sets, but free options exist
- Warren Huart won 2019 TEC Award for Audio Education

**MixOff Community**
- https://mix-off.com
- Forum where members upload multitrack sessions for others to download
- No membership required for downloads
- Good for varied, community-sourced material

### Curated Lists & Aggregators

**Behind The Speakers (Jason Moss)**
- https://behindthespeakers.com/300-free-multitracks/
- Curated list of 300+ free multitracks from multiple sources across genres
- Links to Cambridge-MT, Telefunken, Shaking Through, pureMix, and more

**Subaqueous Music**
- https://www.subaqueousmusic.com/400-free-multitracks-to-level-up-you-mixing-skills/
- Guide aggregating 400+ free multitracks from various sites

### Additional Sources

- **SongStems.net** — sharing platform for remix packs, stems, acapellas, instrumentals
- **Isolated-Tracks.com** — stems across styles including retro jazz, rock, pop (396 jazz songs)
- **Tosky Records** — free high-quality multitrack (Pro Tools compatible, 1.3GB) of jazz quartet for educational use
- **Ultimate Metal Forum** — comprehensive DI download list for metal/rock mixing practice (https://ultimatemetal.com/threads/free-multitracks-dis-download-list.1514051/)
- **MxU / Thrive Worship** — free worship/gospel multitracks for practice
- **Weathervane Music "Shaking Through"** — documentary series with multitrack access (some free, some membership-required)
- **Splice** (acquired Indaba Music) — remix contest stems; Rubber Tracks Sample Library has 40,000+ royalty-free samples

### Academic Research Datasets

**MUSDB18 / MUSDB18-HQ**
- 150 full-length music tracks (~10 hours), multiple genres
- Isolated stems: drums, bass, vocals, other
- Stereo, 44.1kHz; HQ version is uncompressed WAV
- Available on Zenodo (academic access request required)
- CC BY-NC-SA license for many tracks
- Standard benchmark dataset for source separation research

**MedleyDB / MedleyDB 2.0**
- 196 total multitracks (122 + 74) across 9 genres
- WAV 44.1kHz/16-bit with mix + processed stems + raw audio + metadata
- Melody annotations, instrument activations, genre labels
- Available on Zenodo (permission request)

**MoisesDB**
- 240 tracks from 45 artists, 12 genres
- 3-10 stems per track in hierarchical taxonomy
- Largest publicly available multitrack set beyond 4-stem separation
- Python package for access

**Slakh2100**
- 2,100 automatically mixed tracks from synthesized MIDI
- 187 instrument patches, 34 classes, 145 hours
- Useful for studying automatic mixing approaches

**Open Multitrack Testbed (Queen Mary University of London)**
- http://multitrack.eecs.qmul.ac.uk
- Searchable semantic database of multitracks with metadata
- Includes raw tracks, stems, mixes, and DAW files
- Creative Commons licensed content
- Classical collection: Bach (5 multitracks/86 tracks), Beethoven, Ysaÿe, Paganini

## 5. Academic Research on Mixing Pedagogy

### Key Papers & Research Areas

**AES Education Conferences**
- 2013, 2015, 2021, 2023 conferences generated 53+ papers across:
  - Careers and Industry
  - Listening, Mixing and Perception
  - Program Overviews
  - Discipline-Specific Case Studies and Pedagogy
- 2023 conference included papers on observational learning in mixing, critical listening taxonomy
- AES E-Library: 20,000+ searchable PDFs (free for members)

**Perceptual Evaluation of Mixing**
- De Man, Boerum, Leonard, King, Massenburg & Reiss (2015). "Perceptual evaluation of music mixing practices." *AES 138th Convention.*
  - Rated mixes on continuous quality scale; found correlations between sonic features and perceived quality
  - Second-year students' mixes rated higher than first-year, showing measurable skill development
  - Available via ResearchGate

**Critical Listening & Ear Training Research**
- Three most frequent practices: verbal communication, sequencing (adaptive difficulty), software-based technical ear training
- Technical ear training proven effective for junior audio engineers
- Research traces from Miskiewicz's pioneering work through current interactive tools
- Audio engineering schools increasingly emphasizing "listening skills" and "musical training" in curricula

**Intelligent / Automatic Mixing**
- Lefford, Bromham, Fazekas & Moffat (2021). "Context-Aware Intelligent Mixing Systems." *JAES.*
- Moffat (2019). "Approaches in Intelligent Music Production" — defines IMP, surveys knowledge-based vs. ML approaches
- csteinmetz1's AutomaticMixingPapers repo tracks the full field

**Expert-Novice Differences in Mixing**
- Research shows experts build interconnected knowledge networks vs. novices' isolated facts
- Experts attend to relevant cues more quickly with selective attention
- No specific eye-tracking studies in audio mixing found (gap in literature)

**Mixing Pedagogy Best Practices**
- Courses should include both theory and practical demonstration
- Start with fundamentals: balance without effects, then layered processing
- Observational learning from masterclasses transfers technical, musical, and pedagogical elements
- Democratization of technology demands curricula linking creative practice with commercial production

**Open Access Textbooks/Course Materials**
- MIT OCW 21M.380 (full lecture notes and assignments)
- MERLOT, OER Commons, MusOPEN for music/audio resources
- Routledge companion websites for audio engineering textbooks

## 7. Recommended Starting Points for a Classroom

For an undergraduate lecture on mixing, these resources form a strong open-access foundation:

1. **Multitrack source material**: Cambridge-MT (500+ tracks, classroom-ready excerpts)
2. **Structured theory**: MIT OCW 21M.380 + iZotope Learn Hub guides
3. **Video demonstrations**: Waves "From Demo to Master" + Dan Worrall's deep-dive tutorials
4. **Hands-on practice**: SoundGym free tier for ear training + Critical Listening Lab
5. **Interactive/code-based**: Josh Reiss's Web Audio API demos for in-browser exploration; EarSketch (Georgia Tech) for coding + music
6. **Python-based mixing**: Spotify's Pedalboard for effects in Python; pymixconsole for virtual mixing console signal flow
7. **Assessment framework**: De Man et al. perceptual evaluation research for understanding how to evaluate student mixes
8. **Free DAW options**: REAPER (free license for education), Audacity, Ardour, openDAW (browser-based, education-focused), or GridSound
