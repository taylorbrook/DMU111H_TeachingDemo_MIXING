2. FREE VIDEO COURSES FROM ACADEMIC PLATFORMS
================================================================================

A. COURSERA - BERKLEE MUSIC PRODUCTION SPECIALIZATION
   - URL: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/music-production
   - Provider: Berklee College of Music
   - Structure: 4 courses + capstone:
     (1) The Art of Music Production
     (2) The Technology of Music Production
     (3) Pro Tools Basics
     (4) Music Production Capstone
   - Mixing content: Course 2 covers recording, editing, mixing; hardware/
     software mixing boards (volume, pan, mute, solo, busses, inserts, sends,
     submixes); compression, equalization, delay, and audio effects
   - Free access: Can audit individual courses for free (access all content
     except graded items). Free preview of first module. 7-day free trial for
     full specialization. Financial aid available
   - Production quality: High -- professional Berklee faculty, structured
     curriculum
   - Target audience: Beginners to intermediate
   - Notable: From one of the world's premier music schools

B. COURSERA - MIXING AND COMPLETING YOUR MUSIC PRODUCTION
   - URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/mixing-and-completing-your-music-production
   - Provider: GRAMMY GO
   - Structure: 3 modules:
     (1) Post-Production (9 videos, 10 readings, 3 assignments)
     (2) Mixing (EQ strategies, balancing, emotional/sonic characteristics)
     (3) Finalization (organizing work, delivery standards, formats)
   - Free access: Can enroll for free (audit mode)
   - Production quality: High -- GRAMMY organization production values
   - Target audience: Beginner to intermediate
   - Notable: Emphasis on collaborative practices, cross-border work,
     effective communication; practical demo mix creation

C. COURSERA - THE TECHNOLOGY OF MUSIC PRODUCTION
   - URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/technology-of-music-production
   - Provider: Berklee College of Music
   - Content: Music production process (recording, editing, mixing), mixing
     board functionality, compression, equalization, delay, audio effects
   - Free access: Audit for free by selecting "Audit" at enrollment
   - Production quality: High
   - Target audience: Beginners

D. MIT OPENCOURSEWARE - 21M.380 MUSIC AND TECHNOLOGY
   - URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/21m-380-music-and-technology-recording-techniques-and-audio-production-fall-2016/
   - Instructor: Dr. Florian Hollerweger (Fall 2016)
   - Topics: Physical nature and perception of sound, microphones and
     loudspeakers, mixing consoles, signal processors, DAWs, microphone
     selection/positioning, editing, mixing, mastering, dynamics and
     compression, stereo recording, digital audio, room acoustics, reverberation
   - Available materials: Lecture notes (comprehensive), demonstration audio
     files, exams with solutions, readings, assignments, quizzes
   - Free access: Completely free and open access (Creative Commons license)
   - IMPORTANT NOTE: No video lectures available -- text-based lecture notes
     and audio demonstrations only
   - Production quality: Academic rigor; excellent written materials
   - Target audience: Undergraduate level; intermediate depth
   - Notable: Combines practical and theoretical perspectives from MIT

E. KADENZE - SOUND PRODUCTION IN ABLETON LIVE
   - URL: https://www.kadenze.com/courses/sound-production-in-ableton-live-for-musicians-and-artists/info
   - Provider: California Institute of the Arts
   - Topics: Producing, mixing, sound design; fundamentals of sound/digital
     audio, gain staging, distortion in recordings/mixes, levels and panning,
     sub-group mixing, EQ, compression, delay, reverb
   - Free access: Can audit course content for free (premium features require
     subscription). Note: enrollment may be session-based
   - Target audience: Musicians and artists new to DAW-based production

F. KADENZE - EXPERIMENTAL FOUNDATIONS FOR SOUND DESIGN
   - URL: https://www.kadenze.com/courses/experimental-foundations-for-sound-design/info
   - Provider: Columbia University Music Center
   - Topics: Musique Concrete and Elektronische Musik techniques applied to
     modern platforms, large-scale layering, frequency distribution, EQ and
     compression as orchestration tools
   - Free access: Free audit available (first Kadenze course to offer this)
   - Target audience: Intermediate; creative/experimental approach

G. KADENZE - ELECTRONIC MUSIC PRODUCTION WITH JOHN TEJADA
   - URL: https://www.kadenze.com/courses/electronic-music-production-with-john-tejada/info
   - Topics: Sound design, arranging, mixing, mastering
   - Free access: Audit option available
   - Target audience: Electronic music producers
   - Notable: Project-driven; Tejada has 25+ years experience

H. UDEMY - AUDIO ENGINEERING: EQ AND COMPRESSION FOR BEGINNERS
   - URL: https://www.udemy.com/course/audio-engineering-eq-and-compression-for-beginners/
   - Content: Understanding EQ and compression, practical applications
   - Free access: Completely free course
   - Target audience: Beginners
   - Notable: Instructor has 15+ years experience

SUMMARY: TOP RECOMMENDATIONS BY AUDIENCE LEVEL

FOR ABSOLUTE BEGINNERS:
1. Coursera: "The Technology of Music Production" (Berklee) -- free audit
