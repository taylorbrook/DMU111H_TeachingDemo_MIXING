FREE MULTITRACK MIX SESSIONS AND STEMS FOR TEACHING AUDIO MIXING
==================================================================
Compiled: March 2026

This document catalogs open-access and free multitrack resources suitable
for teaching audio mixing at the undergraduate level.

1.1 CAMBRIDGE-MT / MIXING SECRETS FREE MULTITRACK DOWNLOAD LIBRARY
    URL: https://cambridge-mt.com/ms3/mtk/
    Run by: Mike Senior (Reviews Editor, Sound On Sound magazine;
            author of "Mixing Secrets For The Small Studio")

    What's available:
    - 500+ full-length multitrack projects across a wide range of genres
    - Genres include pop, rock, country, folk, hip hop, R&B, singer-songwriter,
      classical, electronica, jazz, world, metal, punk, and more
    - Each project is a ZIP archive of uncompressed WAV files
      (24-bit or 16-bit, 44.1 kHz)
    - Audio is provided "raw" -- no effects or processing beyond what was
      printed during tracking/editing
    - Many projects also have "Edited Excerpt" quick-download versions
      (typically the biggest chorus) -- well suited for classroom assignments
    - "Suggestions for Newbies" page curates less-challenging projects:
      https://cambridge-mt.com/ms3/mtk-newbies/
    - Also offers a "Recording Secrets" backing-stems library:
      https://cambridge-mt.com/rs1/bkg/

    Licensing:
    - Free for educational use and personal mixing practice
    - Not for commercial use without permission from copyright holders

    Educational suitability: EXCELLENT
    - Explicitly designed for music-technology students and educators
    - Bite-sized excerpts are ideal for in-class mixing exercises
    - Segregated by genre for easy assignment design
    - The single most comprehensive free resource available

SECTION 2: ACADEMIC / RESEARCH DATASETS
================================================================================

2.1 MUSDB18 / MUSDB18-HQ
    URL: https://sigsep.github.io/datasets/musdb.html
    Download: https://zenodo.org/records/1117372 (MUSDB18)
              https://zenodo.org/records/3338373 (MUSDB18-HQ)

    What's available:
    - 150 full-length music tracks (~10 hours total)
    - Split: 100 training / 50 testing
    - 4 isolated stems per track: drums, bass, vocals, other
    - MUSDB18: Native Instruments STEMS format (.mp4), AAC @256kbps,
      stereo, 44.1 kHz
    - MUSDB18-HQ: Uncompressed WAV files, stereo, 44.1 kHz (22.7 GB)
    - Compiled from DSD100, MedleyDB, NI stems pack, and other sources
    - Multiple genres

    Licensing:
    - Academic use only
    - Many tracks under CC BY-NC-SA license
    - Access requires permission request on Zenodo

    Educational suitability: GOOD
    - Standard benchmark dataset in source separation research
    - Only 4 stems (not full multitracks), which limits mixing exercises
      but is useful for teaching about stems/submixes


2.2 MedleyDB / MedleyDB 2.0
    URL: https://medleydb.weebly.com/
    Download: https://zenodo.org/record/1649325 (MedleyDB)
              https://zenodo.org/record/1715175 (MedleyDB 2.0)

    What's available:
    - MedleyDB 1.0: 122 multitracks
    - MedleyDB 2.0: 74 additional multitracks (196 total)
    - Full-length tracks (3-5 minutes), ~7+ hours total
    - Includes: mix + processed stems + raw audio + metadata
    - WAV files at 44.1 kHz, 16-bit
    - Melody f0 annotations (108 tracks), instrument activations,
      genre labels
    - 9 genres: Singer/Songwriter, Classical, Rock, World/Folk, Fusion,
      Jazz, Pop, Musical Theatre, Rap
    - 52 instrumental tracks + 70 vocal tracks

    Licensing:
    - Academic use; permission request required on Zenodo

    Educational suitability: EXCELLENT
    - True multitracks with raw audio (not just stems)
    - Wide genre coverage
    - Rich metadata and annotations for analysis exercises


2.3 MoisesDB
    URL: https://github.com/moises-ai/moises-db
    Paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15913
    Download: https://huggingface.co/datasets/wearemusicai/moisesdb

    What's available:
    - 240 tracks from 45 artists across 12 genres
    - Beyond 4-stem separation: 3-10 stems per track
    - Two-level hierarchical taxonomy of stems (11 top-level categories
      with sub-stems)
    - Includes vocals, drums, bass, guitar, keys, wind instruments, etc.
    - Python package for metadata parsing, stem construction, mixing, I/O

    Licensing:
    - CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-
      ShareAlike 4.0 International)

    Educational suitability: EXCELLENT
    - More granular stem separation than MUSDB18
    - Large and diverse dataset
    - Well-documented with Python tools for programmatic access
    - Suitable for both mixing education and computational research


2.4 THE OPEN MULTITRACK TESTBED
    URL: http://multitrack.eecs.qmul.ac.uk/
    Origin: Queen Mary University of London, Centre for Digital Music

    What's available:
    - Online repository of multitrack audio, mixes, and DAW files
    - Semantic database with rich metadata (search by genre, instrumentation,
      recording conditions, etc.)
    - Includes raw tracks, stems, multiple mixes per song, and DAW session files
    - Classical music batch downloads:
      * J.S. Bach: 5 multitracks, 86 tracks
      * Beethoven: 2 multitracks, 18 tracks
      * Ysaye and Paganini: 5 multitracks, 118 tracks
    - Indexes content from MedleyDB and other sources

    Licensing:
    - Creative Commons licensed content (some allowing commercial use)
    - Contributions from educational institutions and individuals

    Educational suitability: EXCELLENT
    - Unique: provides DAW session files, not just raw audio
    - Multiple mixes of the same song enable comparative analysis
    - Semantic search for finding material matching specific teaching needs
    - Classical music multitracks are rare and valuable for teaching


2.5 SLAKH2100 (SYNTHESIZED LAKH DATASET)
    URL: http://www.slakh.com/

    What's available:
    - 2100 automatically mixed multi-track audio files with aligned MIDI
    - Synthesized from Lakh MIDI Dataset using professional-grade
      virtual instruments
    - 187 instrument patches in 34 classes
    - 145 hours of mixture data
    - Larger than all other open multi-track source separation datasets combined

    Licensing:
    - Academic research use

    Educational suitability: MODERATE
    - Synthesized (not real recordings), so less suitable for teaching
      traditional mixing
    - Valuable for teaching about MIDI, virtual instruments, and
      computational approaches to mixing


2.6 MIXING SECRETS DATASET (ACADEMIC VERSION)
    URL: https://github.com/felixCheungcheung/mixing_secrets_v2

    What's available:
    - Academic dataset derived from Cambridge-MT multitrack library
    - Structured for instrument recognition and source separation research
    - Includes metadata annotations

    Licensing:
    - Academic use

    Educational suitability: GOOD
    - Bridges educational mixing practice and research applications

3.2 GENERAL NOTE ON DAW COMPATIBILITY
    Most free multitrack resources provide raw WAV files in ZIP archives,
    which can be imported into ANY DAW (Pro Tools, Logic Pro, Ableton Live,
    Reaper, Studio One, Cubase, FL Studio, etc.). This is actually
    preferable for teaching, as it is DAW-agnostic and students can use
    whatever software they have access to.

SECTION 8: RECOMMENDATIONS FOR TEACHING
================================================================================

TOP PICKS FOR UNDERGRADUATE MIXING INSTRUCTION:

1. CAMBRIDGE-MT (Primary resource)
   - Largest free library (500+ songs)
   - Explicitly designed for classroom use with bite-sized excerpts
   - Genre diversity allows assignments across musical styles
   - Raw, unprocessed audio teaches students to make all mixing decisions
   - Already widely used in university audio programs worldwide

2. TELEFUNKEN "LIVE FROM THE LAB" (Live recording mixing)
   - Live sessions with mic bleed teach real-world challenges
   - High recording quality
   - Good for intermediate/advanced students

3. MedleyDB (Research-informed teaching)
   - True multitracks with rich metadata
   - Genre diversity
   - Bridges mixing practice and MIR (Music Information Retrieval) research

4. MoisesDB (Advanced/computational)
   - Granular stem taxonomy beyond the standard 4-stem model
   - Python tools enable computational mixing exercises
   - CC BY-NC-SA license is clear and permissive for education

5. OPEN MULTITRACK TESTBED (Comparative mixing analysis)
   - DAW session files enable studying professional mixing decisions
   - Multiple mixes of the same song for A/B comparison exercises
   - Searchable metadata

6. LEWITT AUDIO (Supplementary)
   - Professional studio recordings
   - Good variety

SUGGESTED ASSIGNMENT DESIGN:

- Use Cambridge-MT "Edited Excerpts" for in-class mixing exercises
  (15-30 minute focused activities)
- Use full Cambridge-MT sessions for take-home mixing assignments
- Use Telefunken sessions for live-recording mixing units
- Use MUSDB18/MedleyDB for computational audio or source separation units
- Use the Open Multitrack Testbed for mixing analysis/comparison assignments
- Use MoisesDB for advanced stem-based production exercises


================================================================================
NOTES ON LICENSING
================================================================================

GENERAL PRINCIPLE: Nearly all free multitrack resources are offered
under "educational use only" or "non-commercial" terms. This means:

- Students CAN download, mix, and submit for coursework
- Students CAN use mixes on personal demo reels/portfolios (usually)
- Students CANNOT release mixes commercially
- Students CANNOT upload mixes to streaming platforms for profit
- Instructors SHOULD inform students of these restrictions

The academic datasets (MUSDB18, MedleyDB, MoisesDB) typically require
a brief access request through Zenodo and are restricted to academic use.

Creative Commons licensed content (especially in the Open Multitrack Testbed
and MoisesDB) provides the clearest and most permissive terms for
educational use.
