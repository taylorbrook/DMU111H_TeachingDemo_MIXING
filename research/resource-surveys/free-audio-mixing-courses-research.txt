1. COURSERA COURSES (Free Audit Options)
================================================================================

A) Music Production Specialization (Berklee College of Music)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/music-production
   Provider: Berklee College of Music
   Topics: Recording, editing, mixing with reverb/EQ/compression, Pro Tools,
           microphone techniques, gain staging
   Courses in specialization (4 courses, ~18 weeks total):
     1. The Art of Music Production
     2. The Technology of Music Production
     3. Pro Tools Basics
     4. Music Production Capstone
   Free/Paid: Free to audit (view all content); pay for certificate & graded work
   Quality: High -- Berklee is a world-renowned music institution
   Certificate: Yes, if you pay (Coursera Specialization Certificate)
   Notes: No prior experience required. Covers mixing as part of the broader
          production workflow.

B) The Art of Music Production (Berklee)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/producing-music
   Provider: Berklee College of Music
   Topics: Record production, artistic identity, production techniques,
           emotional impact of recordings, reverb/delay/compression
   Free/Paid: Free to audit
   Quality: High
   Certificate: Paid only
   Duration: ~5 weeks

C) The Technology of Music Production (Berklee)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/technology-of-music-production
   Provider: Berklee College of Music
   Topics: How sound becomes audio signals, recording, editing, mixing,
           DAW fundamentals
   Free/Paid: Free to audit
   Quality: High
   Certificate: Paid only

D) Electronic Music Production Specialization (Berklee)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/specializations/electronic-music-production
   Provider: Berklee College of Music
   Topics: Electronic music tools & techniques, Ableton Live, sound design,
           mixing for electronic genres
   Free/Paid: Free to audit individual courses
   Quality: High
   Certificate: Paid only

E) Introduction to Ableton Live (Berklee)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/ableton-live
   Provider: Berklee College of Music
   Topics: MIDI programming, audio recording, warping, processing, looping,
           editing, mixing, performing, file management
   Free/Paid: Free to audit
   Quality: High

F) Mixing and Completing Your Music Production (GRAMMY GO)
   URL: https://www.coursera.org/learn/mixing-and-completing-your-music-production
   Provider: GRAMMY GO (Recording Academy)
   Topics: Post-production evaluation, demo mixing, reference tracks, EQ,
           compression, mixing for emotional/sonic characteristics, finalizing
           mixes, delivery standards and formats
   Instructors: Carolyn Malachi (GRAMMY nominee, Howard Univ. professor),
                featuring Cirkut, Hit-Boy, Stevie Mackey, Harvey Mason Jr.,
                Judith Sherman
   Free/Paid: Free to audit
   Quality: Very high -- taught by GRAMMY winners and nominees
   Certificate: Paid only
   Notes: Part of the larger "Music Production: Crafting Award-Worthy Songs"
          specialization

6. STANFORD CCRMA (Center for Computer Research in Music and Acoustics)
================================================================================

A) MUSIC 320: Introduction to Audio Signal Processing
   URL: https://ccrma.stanford.edu/courses/320/
   Provider: Stanford University
   Topics: Signal processing for audio/computer music, spectra, filters, DFT,
           spectrograms, convolution
   Free/Paid: Course materials freely accessible online
   Quality: Very high -- Stanford academic level
   Certificate: No
   Textbook companion: https://ccrma.stanford.edu/~jos/ (Julius O. Smith's
   online textbooks on signal processing -- free)
   Notes: More technical/mathematical than practical mixing, but foundational
          for understanding what mixing tools do under the hood.

B) Audio Signal Processing for Music Applications
   URL: https://online.stanford.edu/courses/sohs-ymusic0001-audio-signal-processing-music-applications
   Provider: Stanford / also available on Coursera
   Topics: Audio signal processing, Python-based tools, spectral analysis
   Software: https://github.com/MTG/sms-tools (open source)
   Free/Paid: Materials under open licenses
   Quality: Very high

   URL: https://ccrma.stanford.edu/~jos/
   Provider: Stanford University
   Topics: Mathematics of digital audio signal processing, spectral analysis,

   Free/Paid: Completely free online textbooks

14. FREE CERTIFICATION PROGRAMS
================================================================================

True free certifications in audio engineering are rare. Here is what exists:

A) Alison Diplomas (described above in section 10)
   - Free to study, free digital certificate if scored 80%+
   - Not industry-recognized but documents completion

B) Coursera Certificates
   - Courses can be audited free but certificates require payment
   - Financial aid is available for those who qualify

C) Avid Pro Tools Certification
   URL: https://www.avid.com/learning/certification
   - NOT free (courses $349-$598)
   - Industry-recognized standard
   - Levels: Pro Tools Specialist, Pro Tools Professional
   - Mentioned for completeness since it is the primary industry certification

D) Waves Audio Course Certificates
   URL: https://www.waves.com/courses
   - Some courses are free with certificates
   - Class Central lists 100+ free Waves courses

E) Apple Logic Pro Certification
   - Professional credential from Apple
   - NOT free but worth noting as industry standard

BEST FREE STRUCTURED COURSES (truly free, high quality):
  1. Waves "From Demo to Master" -- free, 8+ hours, GRAMMY winners teaching
  2. Coursera/Berklee Music Production Specialization -- free audit

  1. SoundGym -- gamified ear training + free structured programs

BEST FREE REFERENCE/TEXTBOOK:
  1. iZotope Mixing Guide PDF
  2. Julius O. Smith online books (Stanford) -- for signal processing theory
  3. MIT OCW lecture notes

BEST FREEMIUM (significant free content with paid upgrades):
  1. Kadenze courses (CalArts, Columbia) -- audit free
  2. Berklee Online free resources + Coursera courses
  3. SoundGym free tier
  4. pureMix free tutorials

BEST VALUE IF WILLING TO PAY:
  1. Berklee Online dedicated mixing courses
  2. LinkedIn Learning (often free via public library)
  3. Mix With The Masters (premium)

BEST FOR CERTIFICATES:
  1. Alison (free certificate)
  2. Coursera (paid certificate, financial aid available)
  3. Waves courses (some with free certificates)
  4. Avid Pro Tools Certification (paid, industry standard)
