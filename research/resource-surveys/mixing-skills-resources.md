# Free & Open Access Tools, Apps, and Interactive Resources for Developing Mixing Skills and Critical Listening

Compiled March 2026. All resources verified as available at time of research.

---

## 1. EAR TRAINING PLATFORMS FOR MIXING ENGINEERS

### SoundGym
- **URL:** https://www.soundgym.co/
- **What it offers:** Gamified ear training with mini-games targeting frequency detection, compression identification, panning, reverb, delay, distortion, and filter recognition. Includes 5 free learning programs (Recording & Audio Engineering, Electronic Music Production, Mix & Mastering, Synthesis & Sound Design, Live Sound) and a free audio school with 10-40 hours of tutorials. Social/community features included.
- **Free?** Free basic membership with limited daily plays. Pro plan ($24.95/month, $119/year, or $316 lifetime) unlocks unlimited access to 20+ games.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Assign daily 10-minute ear training sessions; use leaderboards to motivate students; free plan sufficient for introductory exercises. The EQ Playground (https://www.soundgym.co/playground/eq) is entirely free and web-based -- great for in-class demos.

### MixSense
- **URL:** https://mixsense.co/
- **What it offers:** Structured ear training exercises covering EQ (frequency boosts and cuts), compression (attack, release, threshold, ratio), gain/panning, and effects (reverb, distortion, delay). Leveled exercises, daily training sessions, streak tracking, and point systems. Customizable: choose genre and instrument for exercises.
- **Free?** Free to download and use. Premium features may require subscription.
- **Platform:** iOS (App Store) and Android (Google Play)
- **Classroom use:** Assign as homework -- students can train on their phones. Review mastery stats to track progress. Good for students who don't have DAW access at home.

### Critical Listening Lab
- **URL:** https://www.criticallisteninglab.com/en
- **What it offers:** Browser-based interactive exercises for EQ boosts/cuts, compression settings comparison, and spatial audio perception. Learn section with free chapters on EQ fundamentals, compression, reverb, and stereo imaging. Instant feedback, no signup required to try.
- **Free?** Foundations and EQ modules are free. Additional modules may require payment.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based, no plugins or downloads)
- **Classroom use:** Excellent for in-class demonstrations -- runs directly in the browser. No accounts needed to start. Use the Learn modules as assigned reading alongside practical exercises.

### EarQuiz Frequencies
- **URL:** https://earquiz.org/EQ_Frequencies/
- **Source code:** https://github.com/Gdalik/EarQuiz_Frequencies
- **What it offers:** Free, open-source desktop application for frequency band ear training based on David Moulton's Golden Ears methodology. Learn mode (listen to pink noise or music with EQ on/off) and Test mode (guess boosted/cut frequencies, scored out of 10). Supports 1-octave and 1/3-octave bands. Accepts WAV, AIFF, FLAC, MP3, OGG files as audio source. Built-in pink noise generator.
- **Free?** Completely free and open source.
- **Platform:** Windows 10/11, macOS 11+ (Intel and Apple Silicon), Linux
- **Classroom use:** Install on lab computers. Load class-relevant audio material. Use Test mode for graded in-class quizzes. The 1/3-octave mode provides advanced challenges for more experienced students.

## 2. EQ TRAINING TOOLS (FREQUENCY IDENTIFICATION)

### EQ Trainer (eqtrainer.app)
- **URL:** https://eqtrainer.app/
- **What it offers:** Web-based tool that plays EQ'd audio and asks you to identify the boosted frequency. Includes bypass button for A/B comparison. Adjustable Q width for difficulty scaling. Pink Noise mode recommended for beginners.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Zero setup -- open in any browser. Start with Pink Noise mode for beginners, then progress to music. Project on screen for group exercises.

### Berklee PULSE Interactive EQ Ear Trainer
- **URL:** https://pulse.berklee.edu/content/tools/eqTrainer/index.html
- **Portal:** https://pulse.berklee.edu/
- **What it offers:** Free interactive EQ training tool from Berklee College of Music. Identify frequency boosts and cuts. Part of a larger suite of free music education tools (rhythm training, interval training, beat maker, metronome, etc.).
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Berklee brand lends credibility. Use alongside other PULSE tools for a comprehensive music education session. No account required.

### Quiztones
- **URL:** https://theproaudiofiles.com/quiztones/
- **What it offers:** Frequency-altered tones, noise, and musical loops for EQ ear training. Tone Quiz (identify sine wave frequencies) and EQ Quiz (identify boosted/cut frequencies). 450+ quizzes. No visual cues -- trains purely by ear.
- **Free?** Free to start on iOS; Pro subscription ($4.99/month or $19.99/year) for importing audio and Hard/Expert modes. Mac version is a one-time purchase ($19.99).
- **Platform:** iOS, macOS
- **Classroom use:** Good for Apple-equipped labs. The no-visual-cues approach forces genuine ear development.

### Audio Training (Ear training for EQ and Feedback)
- **URL:** https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.saninnsalas.audiotraining
- **What it offers:** Free Android app for frequency recognition practice -- feedback detection training, EQ recognition exercises, compression listening drills. Real-time audio processing. Import your own tracks. Adjustable bandwidth and frequency distribution. Progress tracking.
- **Free?** Free.
- **Platform:** Android
- **Classroom use:** Android-friendly option for students without Apple devices. Use the import feature to load class-specific audio material.

### Parametric EQ Ear Training (Web)
- **URL:** https://ivthefourth.github.io/ear-training-app/
- **What it offers:** Simple, open-source, browser-based parametric EQ ear training tool.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Minimal, distraction-free interface. Good for quick in-class drills.

### earPlugins -- Tune
- **URL:** https://www.earplugins.eu/
- **What it offers:** VST plugin that applies random EQ filters to your audio signal in your DAW; you guess the center frequencies. Immediate auditory and visual feedback.
- **Free?** Free for personal, non-profit use.
- **Platform:** Windows (VST 2.4, 32-bit)
- **Classroom use:** Integrates directly into DAW workflow -- students train while working on actual sessions. Limited to Windows/32-bit, which is a significant constraint.

## 3. WEB-BASED INTERACTIVE MIXING EXERCISES & SIMULATORS

### AudioFusion -- SoundcheckPro
- **URL:** https://audiofusion.com/soundcheckpro/
- **Web App:** https://audiofusion.com/soundcheckpro-web-app/
- **What it offers:** Audio mixer simulator and signal flow training tool. Practice sound engineering "hands-on" without physical hardware. Teaches signal flow of professional mixing consoles. VirtualStudioXR version adds 3D/VR capability. Won AES Silver Award.
- **Free?** SoundcheckPro Web Apps are free educational resources.
- **Platform:** Web (browser, mobile, tablet, Chromebook)
- **Classroom use:** Ideal for teaching signal flow and console operation when physical hardware is unavailable. Runs on Chromebooks (common in educational settings). Use before students access real equipment.

### SAE/Audient ASP4816 Console Simulator
- **URL:** https://audient.com/news/2020/11/16/sae-asp4816-simulator/ (announcement)
- **What it offers:** Photo-realistic, audio-capable simulation of the Audient ASP4816 console. Learn signal flow and practice console operations remotely.
- **Free?** Free and publicly available.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Excellent for teaching analog console signal flow. Photo-realistic interface gives students experience with professional-grade equipment layout.

### Mackie 1604VLZ4 Analog Mixer Simulator
- **URL:** https://mackie.com/en/news/all/SAE_INSTITUTE_1604VLZ4_ANALOG_SIMULATOR.html
- **What it offers:** Digital simulation of the Mackie 1604VLZ4 analog mixer, developed by Dr. Mark Bassett for SAE Creative Media Institute.
- **Free?** Free.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)

### iZotope Pro Audio Essentials
- **URL:** https://pae.izotope.com/

Progressive difficulty, badges, and progress tracking. Developed by Grammy-nominated mastering expert Jonathan Wyner.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)

## 6. FREE SPECTRUM ANALYZERS & METERING TOOLS

### Voxengo SPAN
- **URL:** https://www.voxengo.com/product/span/
- **What it offers:** Professional-grade free real-time FFT spectrum analyzer plugin. Features include correlation meter, EBU R128 metering, output level metering with adjustable ballistics, K-system metering, true peak clipping detection, headroom estimation. CTRL/CMD+drag to audition specific frequency bands.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** macOS (AU, AAX, VST3), Windows (VST, VST3, AAX)
- **Classroom use:** Install on every lab workstation. The frequency band audition feature (CTRL+drag) is invaluable for teaching students to correlate visual and auditory frequency information. Use for A/B demonstrations.

### Youlean Loudness Meter
- **URL:** https://youlean.co/youlean-loudness-meter/
- **What it offers:** LUFS, true peak, and dynamic range metering following EBU R128 standards. Essential for teaching loudness normalization and streaming platform standards.
- **Free?** Free version covers all main measurements. Pro version adds exportable reports and advanced history.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows (AAX, VST, AU)
- **Classroom use:** Teach loudness standards for streaming (Spotify at -14 LUFS, Apple Music at -16 LUFS, YouTube at -14 LUFS). Have students master tracks to target LUFS values and verify with this meter.

### Voxengo AnSpec
- **URL:** https://www.voxengo.com/product/anspec/
- **What it offers:** Free analog-style spectrum analyzer plugin. Smoother, more musical visualization compared to FFT analyzers.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows (VST, VST3, AU, AAX)
- **Classroom use:** Compare side-by-side with SPAN to discuss differences between analog and digital analysis approaches.

### TDR Nova (Free Dynamic EQ)
- **URL:** https://www.tokyodawn.net/tdr-nova/free/
- **What it offers:** While primarily a dynamic EQ, it functions as an excellent visual teaching tool -- 4-band parametric EQ with real-time spectrum display, dynamic EQ visualization, and a drag-and-drop interface. Makes the concept of dynamic EQ immediately visible and understandable.
- **Free?** Completely free. Gentleman's Edition ($60) adds 6 bands and more features.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows (AAX, VST2, VST3, AU)
- **Classroom use:** Best free plugin for teaching the transition from static to dynamic EQ. The visual display makes abstract concepts concrete.

### HoRNet StereoView
- **URL:** https://www.hornetplugins.com/plugins/hornet-stereoview/
- **What it offers:** Free stereo goniometer and correlation meter. Resizable interface. Helps identify phase issues and stereo width.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows (VST, VST3, AU, AAX)
- **Classroom use:** Essential for teaching stereo imaging, phase correlation, and mono compatibility concepts.

### MAAT GON
- **URL:** https://www.maat.digital/gon/
- **What it offers:** Free software goniometer replicating analog oscilloscope behavior. Out-of-phase warning feature.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** macOS (AU, VST2, VST3, AAX), Windows (VST2, VST3, AAX)
- **Classroom use:** Use the out-of-phase warning to demonstrate phase cancellation in real time.

### ToneBoosters GonioMeter
- **URL:** https://www.toneboosters.com/tb_goniometer_v1.html
- **What it offers:** Free real-time visualization of spatial sound field, stereo balance, and correlation spectrum.
- **Free?** Completely free.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows
- **Classroom use:** Visual representation of stereo field helps students understand panning and width concepts.

## 12. AI-POWERED MIXING TOOLS FOR EDUCATIONAL USE

### RoEx Mix Check Studio
- **URL:** https://mixcheckstudio.roexaudio.com/
- **What it offers:** Free AI-powered mix analysis tool. Upload a WAV or MP3, specify genre, and receive detailed feedback on tonal balance, dynamics, loudness, and industry-standard compliance. The Mix Report acts as a learning tool showing how professional mixing techniques are applied.
- **Free?** Free to use. Mastering service has paid tiers.
- **Platform:** Web (browser-based)
- **Classroom use:** Have students upload their mixes and discuss the AI feedback. Compare AI suggestions with instructor feedback. Use as a self-assessment tool before submitting assignments. Great for teaching loudness standards and tonal balance.

### iZotope Neutron Elements (Free)
- **URL:** https://www.izotope.com/en/products/neutron
- **What it offers:** AI-powered mixing assistant that analyzes audio and suggests processing (EQ, compression, etc.). The Elements version includes the AI Assistant that creates a custom signal chain.
- **Free?** Elements version has been offered free periodically. Check current availability -- Native Instruments bundles may include it.
- **Platform:** macOS, Windows (VST, VST3, AU, AAX)
- **Classroom use:** Compare AI suggestions with manual mixing decisions. Use as a teaching moment: "Why did the AI make these choices? Do you agree?" Helps students develop critical evaluation skills.

### Moises AI
- **URL:** https://moises.ai/
- **What it offers:** AI-powered stem separation (vocals, drums, bass, other), pitch/tempo detection, chord detection, and metronome tools. AI mastering with Auto, Advanced, and Reference modes.
- **Free?** Free plan includes basic 2-stem separation (vocals + instrumental), 5 uploads/month, 5-minute file limit. Premium/Pro for advanced separation and mastering.
- **Platform:** iOS, Android, Web
- **Classroom use:** Use stem separation to isolate instruments from commercial mixes for analysis. "How does this kick drum sound in isolation?" Chord detection helps with arrangement discussions. Free tier is sufficient for occasional classroom demonstrations.

### LANDR
- **URL:** https://www.landr.com/
- **What it offers:** AI mastering service. Upload a track and receive an AI-mastered version.
- **Free?** Free low-quality preview. Paid plans for downloads.
- **Platform:** Web, plugin
- **Classroom use:** Compare AI mastering against student mastering attempts. Discuss what the AI does well and where human judgment differs.
