William Moylan — The Art of Recording (2002)
"Mixing is a process that encompasses:
  1. Creating an artistic blend of timbres and dynamic levels, and assigned spatial location, distance, and environment qualities (using the skills and concepts of a traditional composer or orchestrator),
  2. Rehearsing and coordinating the precise changes that will occur to the sound source tracks (functioning similarly to a traditional conductor), and
  3. Actually performing the changes that were determined and rehearsed above in real time (similar to a performance on a traditional musical instrument)."

Wrap-Up Recap

  1. Mixing is an art. Technical skills serve creative vision.
  2. Set up well: organize your session, get your gain staging right, build a static mix before reaching for plugins.
  3. Listen deliberately: take breaks, use references, check on different systems, trust your ears.
  4. Core tools: EQ shapes frequency, compression shapes dynamics, reverb creates space. Each should be used intentionally.
  5. Panning creates your stereo canvas — work with EQ and reverb to give every element its own space.
